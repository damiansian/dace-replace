## Question 1

**Outcome:** 3E.01
**Type:** Recall

**Stem:**
> What does Section 508 primarily govern?

- A. Accessibility requirements for products sold to US federal agencies
- B. Accessibility requirements only for consumer mobile apps
- C. A voluntary design best-practice framework for startups
- D. A global replacement for WCAG

**Correct Answer:** A
**Rationale:** Section 508 is a US federal procurement accessibility requirement. It is especially relevant when products are sold to or used by US federal agencies.

---

## Question 2

**Outcome:** 3E.02
**Type:** Application

**Stem:**
> Which statement best describes EN 301 549?

- A. It is unrelated to WCAG and applies only to printed documents
- B. It is the European harmonized ICT accessibility standard and is often discussed alongside WCAG-based requirements
- C. It applies only to hardware packaging
- D. It eliminates the need to understand European product markets

**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** EN 301 549 is the European harmonized ICT accessibility standard. In practice, accessibility discussions often connect it to WCAG-based conformance and broader European obligations.

## Question 3

**Outcome:** 3E.03
**Type:** Recall

**Stem:**
> Why should Badge 3 learners understand the European Accessibility Act?

- A. Because it removes the need for product-specific accessibility decisions
- B. Because it affects enforcement timelines and market obligations for products in European contexts
- C. Because it applies only to internal Adobe tooling
- D. Because it replaces all other accessibility standards worldwide

**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** The European Accessibility Act matters because it shapes market obligations, enforcement timing, and product planning in European contexts.

---

## Question 4

**Outcome:** 3E.04
**Type:** Application

**Stem:**
> A reviewer says, "This issue is not legally required, but it would significantly reduce user confusion and improve task completion." Which distinction are they making?

- A. The difference between legal risk and best practice
- B. The difference between web and mobile
- C. The difference between engineering and design
- D. The difference between Level A and Level AAA only

**Correct Answer:** A
**Rationale:** Badge 3 learners need to distinguish what the law requires from what is still strongly recommended as accessibility best practice or product-quality improvement.

---

## Question 5

**Outcome:** 3E.04
**Type:** Scenario

**Stem:**
> A team has limited capacity and asks which issue to address first: a clear legal exposure in a regulated market or a non-required improvement with good UX value. What is the strongest initial recommendation?

- A. Always choose the UX improvement first because users notice it more
- B. Address the clear legal exposure first, then sequence the improvement work after risk and user impact are considered together
- C. Treat both items as identical because all accessibility issues are equal
- D. Ignore both until the next major redesign

**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** When capacity is limited, a clear legal exposure usually carries urgency. The correct recommendation still considers user impact and feasibility, but it should not ignore real compliance risk.
