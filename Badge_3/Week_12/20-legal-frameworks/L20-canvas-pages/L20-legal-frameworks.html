Lesson 20: Legal Frameworks

🚀 Deliver Badge ⚖️ Module 3E

This lesson introduces the legal frameworks that shape accessibility obligations across Adobe products and markets. The goal is not compliance theater. The goal is precise, useful understanding of legal context.

Badge 3 learners need to distinguish legal risk from best practice, connect frameworks to product decisions, and communicate those distinctions clearly to teams and stakeholders.

What should you know?

  • You can identify specific accessibility barriers using WCAG 2.2 AA criteria (Badges 1–2)
  • You understand how accessibility issues are prioritized by severity and user impact (L17)
  • You know that accessibility standards exist but have not yet learned which legal frameworks reference them or why that matters for product decisions

Key Concepts

Section 508 and EN 301 549 3E.01 / 3E.02

Section 508 matters in US federal procurement contexts. EN 301 549 matters in European ICT accessibility contexts. Both shape risk, planning, and product expectations differently.

✓ Checkpoint

  • I can distinguish Section 508 from EN 301 549
  • I understand why jurisdiction matters

European Accessibility Act 3E.03

Scope, enforcement, and reach vary — but the obligation is already active.

The EAA affects market obligations and planning in European contexts. Badge 3 learners need to understand its implications well enough to anticipate product impact and explain urgency.

✓ Checkpoint

  • I can explain why the EAA affects product planning timelines for EU-facing products
  • I can describe what the ADA Title II rule changes for public sector customers
  • I understand how enforcement deadlines create remediation urgency that affects my team's roadmap

Legal risk vs best practice 3E.04

Compliance is the floor, not the ceiling. Some issues create immediate legal exposure. Others are still important because they improve usability and product quality. Leaders need to communicate the difference clearly.

✓ Checkpoint

  • I can distinguish legal exposure from broader best practice
  • I can communicate both without fear-based framing
