# L20: Legal Frameworks - Lesson Plan

**Course:** DACE
**Badge:** Deliver
**Delivery:** In-person (instructor-led)
**Duration:** ~30 minutes (Week 12 session: 30 min review + 60 min across L20, L21)
**Outcomes:** 3E.01, 3E.02, 3E.03, 3E.04

---

## Lesson Overview

This lesson gives learners the legal context required for accessibility leadership in Adobe product work. It introduces Section 508, EN 301 549, and the European Accessibility Act, then teaches the practical distinction between legal risk and best practice.

**Why This Matters:** Product teams need accessibility guidance that is both accurate and usable. Badge 3 learners must be able to discuss legal obligations precisely without turning every review into a fear-based compliance exercise.

## Content Outline

### Opening: Why Legal Frameworks Matter (3 min)

- Accessibility leadership requires fluency in both user impact and compliance context
- Legal frameworks shape procurement, market risk, and remediation urgency
- The goal is accurate interpretation, not compliance theater

---

### Section 1: Section 508 and EN 301 549 (10 min)

**Outcomes:** 3E.01, 3E.02

- Section 508 as US federal procurement standard
- EN 301 549 as European harmonized ICT standard
- Relationship to WCAG 2.2 AA
- Why jurisdiction matters in product planning and sales context

---

### Section 2: European Accessibility Act (8 min)

**Outcomes:** 3E.03

- EAA scope and timeline
- Product implications for Adobe in European markets
- Why regulatory change affects planning and documentation

---

### Section 3: Legal Risk vs Best Practice (7 min)

**Outcomes:** 3E.04

- Legal floor vs design quality ceiling
- How to communicate risk without reducing accessibility to compliance only
- Prioritizing remediation by legal risk, user impact, and implementation feasibility

---

### Close (2 min)

- Badge 3 leaders must know which obligations are legally required and which improvements are strategic or experience-driven
- Transition to L21: "Once you understand the legal context, the next question is how to make the business case."
