# L20: Legal Frameworks - Learning Objectives

**Badge Level:** Deliver
**Delivery:** In-person (instructor-led)
**Duration:** ~30 minutes (part of Week 12 session: 2 lessons in 60 min)
**Outcomes Covered:** 3E.01, 3E.02, 3E.03, 3E.04
**Week:** 12 (03/31/26)

---

## Overview

This lesson introduces the legal frameworks that shape accessibility expectations for Adobe products across markets. Learners move beyond technical issue identification and into compliance interpretation, jurisdiction-specific obligations, and the distinction between legal requirements and broader best practice.

L20 is designed to help designers and reviewers speak accurately about legal obligations without turning accessibility into fear-based compliance work. The lesson grounds legal discussion in product decisions, remediation planning, and communication with stakeholders.

---

## Connection to Prior Lessons

- **L01-L16:** Provide the technical accessibility knowledge that legal frameworks often reference through WCAG and related standards.
- **L17:** Introduced impact- and risk-based prioritization. L20 adds the legal context that influences risk discussions.

## WCAG Success Criteria Mapping

L20 is not about introducing new WCAG criteria. It is about understanding how broader legal frameworks depend on WCAG and related standards:

| Framework | Relevance to L20 |
|-----------|------------------|
| WCAG 2.2 AA | Core conformance baseline referenced by many accessibility standards and compliance conversations |
| Section 508 | US federal procurement accessibility obligations |
| EN 301 549 | European harmonized ICT accessibility standard |
| European Accessibility Act | Regulatory framework affecting products in European markets |

## Learning Outcomes

### 3E.01: Apply Section 508 requirements

**Outcome Statement:**
Understand US federal procurement accessibility standards.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand Adobe's global accessibility obligations, including Section 508
- Know how Section 508 requirements apply to Adobe products sold to US federal agencies
- Know the legal landscape and compliance timelines that affect products across jurisdictions

**Skills Component:**
- Apply Section 508 requirements to a product's specific context and use cases
- Map compliance obligations across jurisdictions

**Application Component:**
- Map a product's compliance requirements across Section 508 and related obligations
- Advise product teams on legal risk for specific design decisions

---

### 3E.02: Apply EN 301 549 requirements

**Outcome Statement:**
Understand European harmonized ICT accessibility standard.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand EN 301 549 as the European harmonized standard and its relationship to WCAG
- Understand how legal requirements differ across jurisdictions

**Skills Component:**
- Apply EN 301 549 requirements and understand where they extend beyond WCAG
- Identify gaps between a product's current state and legal requirements

**Application Component:**
- Identify compliance gaps tied to EN 301 549
- Present compliance status and risk in clear, actionable terms

---

### 3E.03: Understand European Accessibility Act implications

**Outcome Statement:**
Know EAA enforcement timelines and requirements for Adobe products.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Know the European Accessibility Act timeline, scope, and implications for Adobe products
- Understand how market presence affects compliance exposure

**Skills Component:**
- Assess EAA implications for a product's European market presence
- Stay current on evolving legal requirements and communicate changes to the team

**Application Component:**
- Explain EAA implications for product planning and remediation sequencing
- Contribute to compliance documentation and VPAT-related workflows

---

### 3E.04: Distinguish legal risk vs best practice

**Outcome Statement:**
Communicate the difference between compliance requirements and recommended improvements.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand the difference between legal risk and best practice and how to communicate each appropriately
- Know how to distinguish between what the law requires and what constitutes best practice beyond compliance

**Skills Component:**
- Distinguish legal risk vs best practice and communicate the distinction to stakeholders
- Prioritize remediation based on legal risk, user impact, and implementation feasibility
- Communicate legal obligations without creating fear-based compliance culture

**Application Component:**
- Advise product teams on legal risk without collapsing all accessibility discussion into compliance-only framing
- Present remediation priorities with a clear separation between floor and ceiling
