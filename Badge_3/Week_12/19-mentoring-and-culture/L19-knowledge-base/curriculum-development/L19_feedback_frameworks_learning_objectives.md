# L19: Mentoring and Culture - Learning Objectives

**Badge Level:** Deliver
**Delivery:** In-person (instructor-led)
**Duration:** ~20 minutes (part of Week 11 session: 3 lessons in 60 min)
**Outcomes Covered:** 3H.01, 3H.02, 3H.03
**Week:** 11 (03/24/26)

---

## Overview

This lesson shifts Badge 3 from individual review practice to broader capability building. Accessibility leadership is not only about making strong calls on your own work. It is also about raising the baseline for other designers, sustaining knowledge across teams, and improving the systems that shape design decisions.

L19 focuses on three forms of impact: mentoring designers at different levels, building communities of practice, and contributing to standards and process improvements that outlast any one person. The core message is that accessibility maturity grows through systems and relationships, not just technical expertise.

---

## Connection to Prior Lessons

L19 builds on the full DACE sequence and depends especially on L17 and L18:

- **L01-L16 (Discover and Define):** Provide the technical content that mentors and communities will need to reinforce.
- **L17 (Independent Review Methodology):** Establishes the reviewer role. L19 expands that role into a longer-term leadership function.
- **L18 (Feedback and Critique):** Develops high-quality feedback. L19 extends that into sustained mentoring, onboarding, and cultural influence.

L19 is where accessibility work becomes organizational rather than individual.

## Learning Outcomes

### 3H.01: Mentor designers at different skill levels

**Outcome Statement:**
Guide Discover and Define Badge designers on accessibility practice.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand how to mentor designers at different skill levels: Discover Badge designers learning fundamentals, Define Badge designers building testing skills, and non-trained designers encountering accessibility for the first time
- Understand the difference between mentoring moments and review moments
- Know the leadership qualities that support effective mentoring: patience, empathy, persistence, resilience

**Skills Component:**
- Mentor designers at different skill levels with approaches tailored to their current understanding
- Demonstrate accessibility leadership qualities in daily interactions and decisions
- Create onboarding pathways that help new team members build accessibility skills progressively

**Application Component:**
- Mentor Discover Badge and Define Badge designers on both technical skills and leadership development
- Develop a personal leadership approach that helps others build confidence and judgment over time

---

### 3H.02: Build accessibility communities of practice

**Outcome Statement:**
Create and sustain groups that advance accessibility knowledge.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Know how to build communities of practice within Adobe around accessibility that sustain focus beyond individual champions
- Understand how to create cultural change beyond individual projects and checklists
- Know how accessibility connects to Adobe's broader DEI goals

**Skills Component:**
- Build communities of practice that sustain accessibility focus across teams and projects
- Connect accessibility work to broader organizational DEI initiatives
- Identify systemic accessibility challenges in organizational processes and culture

**Application Component:**
- Build or contribute to accessibility communities of practice within Adobe
- Connect your accessibility work to Adobe's DEI initiatives and organizational values
- Propose improvements that would raise accessibility quality broadly across teams

---

### 3H.03: Contribute to organizational standards

**Outcome Statement:**
Improve Adobe accessibility documentation and processes.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand how to contribute to Adobe's accessibility standards, processes, and organizational knowledge
- Know how to recognize opportunities to expand accessibility impact beyond your immediate role

**Skills Component:**
- Contribute to organizational standards and process improvements that raise the accessibility baseline
- Document and share learnings that benefit Adobe's broader accessibility community

**Application Component:**
- Document accessibility innovations that could benefit other Adobe products
- Propose organizational improvements that would increase accessibility quality broadly

## Lesson Checkpoints

### Checkpoint 1: Mentoring
- I can distinguish how I would support a Discover, Define, or untrained designer
- I understand the difference between a mentoring moment and a review moment

### Checkpoint 2: Communities of practice
- I can explain why accessibility maturity depends on communities and shared habits, not just individual experts
- I can name at least one way to sustain accessibility focus across teams

### Checkpoint 3: Standards and culture
- I can identify one way to improve documentation, onboarding, or process in my organization
- I understand that accessibility leadership includes system-level contribution, not just personal expertise
