### Slide 4 — Component 4 + 5: What "Good" Looks Like
**Type:** Content (tier styling)
**Layout:** Combined quality tiers for both components — match W11 pattern but compress

```
Levels of quality

Legal & Business Case (C4):
Developing (2): Lists legal requirements and general benefits without product connection.
Proficient (3): Maps legal exposure to specific findings. Business case uses product-relevant data.
Exemplary (4): Quantifies risk or opportunity in terms decision-makers would use.

Product Strategy (C5):
Developing (2): General recommendation not connected to findings or actual dev process.
Proficient (3): Specific, actionable recommendation tied to C1 data and team's real workflow.
Exemplary (4): Shows how the recommendation fits the broader product roadmap.

You don't need to be at a 3 or 4 right now. You need a concrete plan and a start.
```

**Notes:** Since we have two components, combine the tiers onto one slide to save space. Use the three-tier color system (amber/green/blue) for each component in compressed rows.

### Slide 5 — Component 5: Product Strategy Recommendation
**Type:** Content
**Layout:** Left text, right examples card

```
Component 5    Product Strategy Recommendation

This is not "we should be more accessible."
It is a specific, actionable recommendation connected to your product's dev process.

Pick a format:
• Acceptance criteria for a specific feature or component
• Roadmap input — prioritized improvements for next 1-2 quarters
• Early-intervention proposal — catch issues earlier in the pipeline
• Integration recommendation — accessibility checkpoints in existing process

It must be:
✓ Specific (names the actual feature/component/process)
✓ Actionable (someone could read this and know what to do)
✓ Connected (ties back to C1 findings and C4 rationale)
✓ Realistic (sized to what your team could adopt)

Source: L22 (Product Strategy Integration)
LOs: 3G.01, 3G.02, 3G.03
```
