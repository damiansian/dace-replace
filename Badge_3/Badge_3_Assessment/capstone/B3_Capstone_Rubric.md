### Outcome Coverage Note

Competency groups **3C (Complex Scenario Judgment)** and **3D (WCAG Interpretation)** are not assessed through the capstone. These outcomes are knowledge and judgment outcomes best measured through the Deliver Badge quizzes and the DACE certification exam, where scenario-based questions can isolate those skills. The capstone assesses applied practice outcomes that require real product evidence.

## Criterion 1: Problem Definition (Outcomes 3A.01, 3A.02, 3A.03)

**Canvas Link Outcome:** 3A.01 (Conduct systematic accessibility reviews)  
**Weight:** 4 points  
**Source Week:** Week 11  
**Source Lesson:** L17

**Passing threshold: 2 pts. Any score of 1 requires Revise and Resubmit.**

**What We're Assessing:**
- Data grounding: Are findings based on Jira data, dashboard data, or equivalent product evidence?
- Severity classification: Are issues classified using the Sev 1-4 model with user impact descriptions?
- Pattern identification: Does the analysis identify systemic patterns, not just individual defects?
- Product specificity: Is this about the student's actual product, not generic accessibility issues?

| Level | Score | Performance Indicators |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| **Exemplary** | 4 | Findings grounded in specific product data (Jira, dashboard, audit reports) with exact counts and metrics. Issues classified by Sev 1-4 with clear user-impact descriptions for each severity level. Patterns identified across findings that point to systemic root causes (e.g., "three defect classes account for 74% of Sev 1/2 issues"). Connections drawn between upstream process gaps and downstream defect volume. Analysis could inform a real prioritization decision. |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Findings grounded in product data with supporting evidence. Issues classified by severity using the Sev 1-4 model. At least one pattern or root cause identified. Specific to the student's product, not generic. Complete and actionable. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Issues identified but classified by WCAG criterion or category instead of user impact. Data referenced but not specific (e.g., "many issues" without counts). Product context present but thin. Shows understanding of the task but evidence is surface-level. |
| **Incomplete** | 1 | No product data. Issues are generic or hypothetical. No severity classification. Could apply to any product. Missing or disconnected from the student's actual work context. |

## Criterion 2: PEAS Session Reflection (Outcomes 3A.01-03, 3B.01-03)

**Canvas Link Outcome:** 3B.01 (Deliver constructive accessibility feedback)  
**Weight:** 4 points  
**Source Weeks:** 11-14  
**Source Lessons:** L17, L18

**Passing threshold: 2 pts. Any score of 1 requires Revise and Resubmit.**

**What We're Assessing:**
- Session documentation: Is the PEAS session clearly described (mode, AT used, what was tested)?
- Observation quality: Does the reflection document what actually happened, not just what should have happened?
- Product connection: Does the student connect at least one finding to their own product using SBI or COIN?
- Actionable recommendation: Is there a concrete next step for the student's team?

| Level | Score | Performance Indicators |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| **Exemplary** | 4 | Session documented with mode, AT, date, and specific task tested. Observation describes a concrete moment of failure or success in detail (what the user did, what happened, what the user experienced). SBI/COIN connection to the student's product is specific: names the pattern, the component, and the user impact. Recommendation includes a filed ticket or documented action taken. Reflection shows the student's understanding changed as a result of the observation. |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Session documented clearly. Observation describes what happened with enough specificity to be useful. SBI or COIN connection to own product is present and reasonable. Recommendation is concrete and actionable. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Session documented but observation is general ("the user had trouble" without describing what specifically happened). Connection to own product is vague or forced. Recommendation is generic ("we should test more"). |
| **Incomplete** | 1 | Session not documented or reflection is a summary of accessibility concepts rather than an observation. No connection to own product. No actionable recommendation. |

## Criterion 3: Contribution Artifact (Outcomes 3H.01, 3H.02, 3H.03)

**Canvas Link Outcome:** 3H.01 (Mentor designers at different skill levels)  
**Weight:** 4 points  
**Source Week:** Week 11  
**Source Lesson:** L19

**Passing threshold: 2 pts. Any score of 1 requires Revise and Resubmit.**

**What We're Assessing:**
- Team specificity: Is the artifact built for this student's team and their actual patterns?
- Practical utility: Could a teammate use this during real work without additional context?
- Reusability: Is the artifact designed to be used more than once?
- Quality: Is the content accurate and well-organized?

| Level | Score | Performance Indicators |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| **Exemplary** | 4 | Artifact is specific to the team's component patterns, workflows, or tools. Designed for immediate use (not "someday"). Includes context that makes it usable by someone unfamiliar with accessibility (onboarding-quality). Addresses a gap identified elsewhere in the portfolio (e.g., the testing gap from C1 or the pattern from C2). Could be shared beyond the immediate team. |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Artifact is specific to the team's context. Practical and usable during real work. Reusable (not a one-time document). Content is accurate. Clearly organized. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Artifact exists but is generic (could apply to any team). Or: is team-specific but not practical enough to use during actual work. Or: is a one-time document rather than a reusable resource. |
| **Incomplete** | 1 | No artifact, or artifact is a generic accessibility overview copied from external sources. Not connected to the team's actual work. |

## Criterion 4: Legal and Business Case (Outcomes 3E.01-04, 3F.01-03)

**Canvas Link Outcome:** 3F.01 (Build compelling accessibility business cases)  
**Weight:** 4 points  
**Source Week:** Week 12  
**Source Lessons:** L20, L21

**Passing threshold: 2 pts. Any score of 1 requires Revise and Resubmit.**

**What We're Assessing:**
- Legal mapping: Does the student identify which legal frameworks apply to their product segment?
- Exposure specificity: Are open issues connected to specific compliance standards (not just "WCAG")?
- Business framing: Is the case framed for the audience who makes resource decisions?
- Cost of inaction: Does the student articulate what happens if the problem is not addressed?

| Level | Score | Performance Indicators |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| **Exemplary** | 4 | Identifies all applicable legal frameworks (Section 508, EN 301 549, EAA, ADA Title II/III as relevant) with correct scope and enforcement details. Maps specific open issues from C1 to conformance failures in those frameworks. Business case is framed in terms the decision-maker cares about (procurement risk, brand, rework cost, legal exposure) rather than in accessibility jargon. Cost of inaction is concrete and supported by evidence. |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Identifies the major applicable frameworks correctly. Connects findings from C1 to compliance requirements. Business case is present and framed for a non-technical audience. Cost of inaction is addressed. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Frameworks listed but with errors in scope or applicability. Business case is present but framed as an accessibility argument rather than a business argument ("we should care about accessibility" instead of "here is the risk"). Connection to C1 findings is loose. |
| **Incomplete** | 1 | No legal mapping, or frameworks listed without connection to the product. No business case, or case is a generic statement about accessibility importance. |

## Criterion 5: Product Strategy Recommendation (Outcomes 3G.01, 3G.02, 3G.03)

**Canvas Link Outcome:** 3G.02 (Write accessibility acceptance criteria)  
**Weight:** 4 points  
**Source Week:** Week 12  
**Source Lesson:** L22

**Passing threshold: 2 pts. Any score of 1 requires Revise and Resubmit.**

**What We're Assessing:**
- Specificity: Is the recommendation actionable enough to implement without further interpretation?
- Acceptance criteria: Are there testable criteria, not just goals?
- Process integration: Does the recommendation connect to the team's existing workflow?
- Scope: Is the recommendation appropriately scoped (not "fix everything" or "do better")?

| Level | Score | Performance Indicators |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| **Exemplary** | 4 | Recommendation targets a specific process change (acceptance criteria, Definition of Done update, roadmap input, or early-intervention proposal). Includes testable criteria that an engineer could verify. Connected to the team's actual development workflow (names the tools, ceremonies, or artifacts involved). Includes an adoption path with realistic stages. Builds on the evidence from C1 and the case from C4. |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Recommendation is specific and actionable. Includes acceptance criteria or testable requirements. Connected to the product's development process. Appropriately scoped. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Recommendation exists but is vague ("we should add accessibility checks to our process") or disconnected from the team's actual workflow. No testable criteria. May be too broad or too narrow. |
| **Incomplete** | 1 | No recommendation, or recommendation is a general statement about accessibility importance. Not connected to a specific process, team, or product. |

## Criterion 6: Stakeholder Connections (Outcomes 3E.01, 3F.01, 3G.01)

**Canvas Link Outcome:** 3E.01 (Apply Section 508 requirements)  
**Weight:** 4 points  
**Source Weeks:** 11-14  
**Source Lessons:** L20, L21, L22

**Passing threshold: 2 pts. Any score of 1 requires Revise and Resubmit.**

**What We're Assessing:**
- Contact made: Did the student actually reach out to Legal and Program Management counterparts?
- Understanding demonstrated: Does the student describe what these roles cover?
- Organizational insight: Did the student learn something about how accessibility decisions flow?
- Relationship quality: Is this a real connection, not just an email sent?

| Level | Score | Performance Indicators |
|-------|-------|------------------------|
| **Exemplary** | 4 | Contacted both Legal and Program Management counterparts (or equivalent roles). Documents who they are, what their role covers, and how their work connects to the student's product. Describes at least one insight about how accessibility decisions flow through the organization that the student did not know before. Connection informed or shaped another component (e.g., the legal mapping in C4 or the strategy in C5). |
| **Proficient** | 3 | Contacted at least two stakeholders. Documents their roles. Describes at least one thing learned about how accessibility decisions are made. Shows genuine engagement. |
| **Developing** | 2 | Contacted one stakeholder, or contact was minimal (an email exchange without substantive conversation). Documentation is thin. Learning is generic ("I learned Legal handles compliance"). |
| **Incomplete** | 1 | No stakeholder contact documented. Or: documentation is fabricated or describes roles from a website rather than from a conversation. |

## Grading Guidelines

### Scoring Approach

1. **Read the full portfolio first** before scoring any component. The narrative arc matters.
2. **Score each criterion independently** against the performance indicators.
3. **Use whole numbers only** (4, 3, 2, or 1). Unlike lesson rubrics, the capstone does not use sub-ranges within levels.
4. **Check for coherence:** Components should reference each other. C5's strategy should build on C1's problem and C4's case.
5. **The container is flexible.** Score the evidence, not the format. A slide deck, written report, website, or video are all acceptable.

### Feedback Template

For each criterion, provide:
- **Score and level** (e.g., "3 / Proficient")
- **What worked:** Specific evidence the student presented effectively
- **What's missing:** Specific gaps, if any, relative to the next level
- **For revision (if score is 1):** Exactly what must be present in the resubmission

### Revision Policy

- If any component scores 1: **Revise and Resubmit** for the flagged components only
- Feedback provided within one week of submission (by May 2)
- Resubmission deadline: one week after feedback
- Revised score replaces original for the flagged components
