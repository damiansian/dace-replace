Week 15: Final Portfolio Submission

Submit your completed capstone portfolio. This is the Deliver Badge assessment.

🚀 Deliver Badge 📋 Week 15 · Apr 21 🎯 Due: Friday, April 25

This is it. Your capstone portfolio is the evidence that you can identify accessibility problems in your product, build the case for addressing them, contribute to your team's capability, and recommend a path forward. Submit your portfolio as a single URL by Friday, April 25.

📦 What to Submit

One URL that contains your complete capstone portfolio with all six components.

Your portfolio must include:

C1: Problem Definition Data-driven analysis of your product's accessibility issues, classified by severity
C2: PEAS Session Reflection Structured reflection connecting PEAS findings to your product
C3: Contribution Artifact Reusable resource tailored to your team, with context note
C4: Legal and Business Case Legal exposure mapped, business rationale framed for decision-makers
C5: Product Strategy Recommendation Specific, actionable recommendation connected to your product's dev process
C6: Stakeholder Connections Documentation of Legal and PM introductions and what you learned

Acceptable formats: Slide deck, written report, narrative website, video walkthrough, or a combined format. The container is flexible — the evidence is not. Every format must include all six components with enough depth to evaluate against the rubric.

📏 Evaluation Rubric Reminder

Each component is scored 1-4. You need a 2 or higher on every component to pass. The full rubric is on the capstone page — review it before you submit.

Score Level What It Means
4 Exemplary Exceeds expectations. Could be used as a model.
3 Proficient Meets all expectations. Complete, specific, grounded in real data.
2 Developing Meets minimum. Present but may be generic or loosely connected.
1 Incomplete Does not meet expectations. Missing, generic, or disconnected.
20-24 points Pass with Distinction
16-19 points Pass with Merit
12-15 points Pass
Any component at 1 Revise and Resubmit

✅ Before You Submit: Final Checklist

  • All six components are present and clearly labeled
  • Each component has enough depth to evaluate against the rubric (not just a placeholder or outline)
  • Your problem definition (C1) uses real data and classifies findings by severity
  • Your PEAS reflection (C2) connects findings to your product using a feedback framework
  • Your contribution artifact (C3) is specific to your team, not generic
  • Your legal and business case (C4) connects to your specific findings and stakeholders
  • Your recommendation (C5) is specific, actionable, and tied to your C1 data
  • Your stakeholder connections (C6) are documented with what you learned
  • The portfolio tells a coherent story — the components connect, not just coexist
  • Your URL works and the content is accessible to the evaluator

📤 Submission

Due: Friday, April 25
