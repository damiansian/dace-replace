# L18: Feedback and Critique - Learning Objectives

**Badge Level:** Deliver
**Delivery:** In-person (instructor-led)
**Duration:** ~20 minutes (part of Week 11 session: 3 lessons in 60 min)
**Outcomes Covered:** 3B.01, 3B.02, 3B.03
**Week:** 11 (03/24/26)

---

## Overview

This lesson focuses on the delivery side of accessibility leadership. By Badge 3, learners can identify issues and reason about accessibility quality. L18 develops the communication discipline required to make that expertise useful to other people. The central question is not just "What is wrong?" but "How do I help someone understand it, act on it, and stay engaged?"

The lesson introduces structured feedback frameworks, critique facilitation patterns, and communication adjustments for different levels of accessibility maturity. It positions feedback as capability-building work, not just error reporting.

---

## Connection to Prior Lessons

L18 builds on the technical foundation established in Badges 1 and 2 and the review methodology introduced in L17:

- **L01-L08 (Discover):** Learners practiced identifying issues and making accessibility requirements visible through annotations. L18 shifts from identifying issues to discussing them productively.
- **L09-L16 (Define):** Learners built enough technical fluency to explain screen reader, voice control, magnification, WHCM, user preference, and cognitive-accessibility issues. L18 is about translating that fluency into effective critique.
- **L17 (Independent Review Methodology):** L17 defined how to conduct a review. L18 focuses on how to communicate findings so they lead to change rather than defensiveness.

L18 is the communication layer on top of L17's review methodology.

## Learning Outcomes

### 3B.01: Deliver constructive accessibility feedback

**Outcome Statement:**
Provide specific and actionable and kind feedback on accessibility issues.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand the anatomy of effective feedback: specific, actionable, user-focused, and kind
- Know structured feedback frameworks: SBI (Situation-Behavior-Impact) and COIN (Context-Observation-Impact-Next)
- Understand when to ask questions vs. give answers and how questioning builds understanding
- Know how to give hard feedback kindly without creating unnecessary defensiveness

**Skills Component:**
- Deliver specific, actionable feedback that identifies both problems and paths forward
- Use SBI and COIN to structure accessibility observations and recommendations
- Ask diagnostic questions that help designers discover issues themselves
- Deliver difficult feedback with clarity while maintaining respect

**Application Component:**
- Provide written feedback on blueline annotations and design work using established frameworks
- Mentor designers through accessibility challenges using questioning techniques that build understanding
- Adapt feedback based on audience and context

---

### 3B.02: Facilitate accessibility critique sessions

**Outcome Statement:**
Lead group reviews with appropriate structure and participation management.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand accessibility critique session structure: timing, participation patterns, and facilitation techniques
- Know Adobe's critique culture and how accessibility reviews integrate with existing design critique practices
- Understand psychological safety and its role in productive feedback sessions
- Recognize defensive reactions and know de-escalation strategies

**Skills Component:**
- Facilitate accessibility critique sessions with appropriate structure and timing
- Manage participation so critique sessions stay focused and productive
- Build psychological safety in review sessions so designers feel comfortable asking questions
- Manage defensive reactions by reframing feedback as user advocacy rather than personal criticism

**Application Component:**
- Facilitate PEAS critique sessions for your product team
- Create a feedback culture where accessibility reviews are seen as helpful, not punitive
- Document effective critique patterns from experienced reviewers and adapt them to your own facilitation

---

### 3B.03: Adapt communication for different skill levels

**Outcome Statement:**
Adjust feedback approach based on recipient accessibility knowledge level.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Know how to adapt communication for Discover Badge designers, Define Badge designers, and non-trained designers
- Understand the difference between mentoring moments and review moments in feedback conversations
- Understand how to receive feedback professionally when your own reviews are being reviewed

**Skills Component:**
- Adapt communication style based on the designer's accessibility knowledge level
- Distinguish between mentoring moments and review moments in feedback conversations
- Receive feedback about your own reviews professionally and incorporate it into practice

**Application Component:**
- Model professional feedback reception when your own work is reviewed
- Adapt your feedback approach based on audience, context, and current skill level
- Continue refining your critique style by tracking what helps different audiences act effectively

## Lesson Checkpoints

### Checkpoint 1: Constructive feedback
- I can distinguish specific, actionable feedback from vague criticism
- I can explain when SBI or COIN would improve the structure of my comments

### Checkpoint 2: Critique facilitation
- I understand how to structure a short accessibility critique so it stays focused and psychologically safe
- I can identify at least one strategy for de-escalating a defensive reaction

### Checkpoint 3: Audience adaptation
- I can adjust my communication for designers with different accessibility skill levels
- I understand the difference between a mentoring moment and a review moment
