🧩 Assembling Your Portfolio

The six components are the evidence. Your portfolio ties them into a narrative:

  1. Here is the problem my product faces (Component 1: Problem Definition)
  2. Here is what I observed in practice (Component 2: PEAS Reflection)
  3. Here is what I contributed to my team (Component 3: Contribution Artifact)
  4. Here is the case for action (Component 4: Legal and Business Case)
  5. Here is what I recommend (Component 5: Product Strategy Recommendation)
  6. Here is who I connected with to make it happen (Component 6: Stakeholder Connections)

You do not need to present them in this order. Arrange them in whatever sequence tells your product's story most effectively. The container is flexible — the evidence is not.

🔍 Before You Submit: Quality Check

For each component, check:

  • Is it specific to my product? Generic content that could describe any product will score lower. Names, data, real examples matter.
  • Is the evidence grounded? Data from Jira, dashboards, or real conversations — not assumptions or general statements.
  • Does it connect to the rest? Each component should reference or build on others. The problem definition should inform the business case. The recommendation should address the problem.
  • Would someone outside my team understand it? Your evaluator knows accessibility but does not know your product. Provide enough context.
  • Does it meet at least a 2 on the rubric? Review the rubric on the capstone overview page. Every component needs a 2 or higher to pass.
