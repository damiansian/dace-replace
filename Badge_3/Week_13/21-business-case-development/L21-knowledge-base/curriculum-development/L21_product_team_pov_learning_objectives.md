# L21: Business Case Development - Learning Objectives

**Badge Level:** Deliver
**Delivery:** In-person (instructor-led)
**Duration:** ~30 minutes (part of Week 12 session: 2 lessons in 60 min)
**Outcomes Covered:** 3F.01, 3F.02, 3F.03
**Week:** 12 (03/31/26)

---

## Overview

This lesson develops the business argument for accessibility work. Badge 3 learners are expected to move beyond identifying issues and into making the case for investment, sequencing, and leadership attention. The lesson focuses on user reach, legal exposure, brand value, efficiency gains, and the cost of inaction.

---

## Learning Outcomes

### 3F.01: Build compelling accessibility business cases

**Outcome Statement:** Connect accessibility investments to business goals leadership cares about.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Know how to build business cases for accessibility investments: user reach, legal risk, brand value, efficiency gains
- Understand accessibility's role in brand positioning, values, and market differentiation
- Understand how accessibility investments compound over time through reduced rework and improved processes

**Skills Component:**
- Build compelling business cases that connect accessibility to business goals leadership cares about
- Quantify the cost of accessibility debt to support proactive investment

**Application Component:**
- Draft business cases for accessibility improvements in your product area
- Present accessibility investments to skeptical stakeholders with compelling rationale

---

### 3F.02: Communicate ROI to leadership

**Outcome Statement:** Present accessibility metrics and impact in business terms.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand how to communicate accessibility value to leadership in business terms they care about
- Know how to measure accessibility impact over time to demonstrate ROI

**Skills Component:**
- Communicate ROI to leadership using metrics and language that resonate with business stakeholders
- Present accessibility metrics in ways that drive continued investment

**Application Component:**
- Track accessibility quality over time and report on trends to leadership
- Create dashboards or reports that make accessibility progress visible

---

### 3F.03: Connect accessibility to business goals

**Outcome Statement:** Frame accessibility work in terms of user reach and legal risk and brand value.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Know how to connect accessibility improvements to revenue, retention, customer satisfaction, legal exposure, and brand value
- Understand the cost of inaction: technical debt, legal exposure, customer churn, and brand damage
- Know how to frame accessibility as a competitive advantage rather than a compliance burden

**Skills Component:**
- Connect accessibility improvements to specific business goals
- Identify which business-case components are strongest for a given situation
- Construct a complete case by combining lenses, metrics, and cost-of-inaction

**Application Component:**
- Connect product accessibility work to broader business strategy
- Draft a complete business case for an accessibility investment in your product area

---
