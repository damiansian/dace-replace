Lesson 21: Business Case Development

🚀 Deliver Badge 📈 Module 3F

Strong accessibility work still needs budget, sequencing, and leadership attention. This lesson focuses on how to make accessibility work legible in business terms without losing the user impact that justifies the work in the first place.

Badge 3 learners need to explain why accessibility matters to product outcomes, risk reduction, customer trust, and operational efficiency. If the case is vague, the work gets deferred.

Key Concepts

Business case structure 3F.01

A strong accessibility business case usually includes user reach, legal risk, brand value, and efficiency gains. The exact balance changes by context, but vague moral claims are not enough for prioritization conversations.

The cost of inaction also matters. Delayed remediation increases rework, support burden, customer frustration, and organizational debt.

✓ Checkpoint

  • I can structure a business case around more than one value dimension
  • I can explain the cost of inaction in product terms

ROI and metrics 3F.02

Useful ROI communication tracks change over time. That might include issue severity trends, time to remediation, customer support burden, or reduction in late-stage rework.

Leadership language is not the same as implementation language. "Reduced critical barriers in a core workflow" will usually land better than "improved semantic structure" even when both statements describe the same work.

✓ Checkpoint

  • I can identify useful ROI signals for accessibility work
  • I can translate technical progress into leadership language

Case construction 3F.03

Not every case leads with the same lens. When legal exposure is high, lead with compliance risk and cost of remediation. When legal exposure is low, lead with user reach and product quality. The four lenses stay available — the emphasis shifts based on the situation.

A complete business case combines three layers: business-case lenses (user reach, legal risk, brand value, efficiency), ROI signals (trend, product, and process metrics), and the cost of inaction (what gets more expensive if deferred). If any layer is missing, the case is easier to defer.

✓ Checkpoint

  • I can explain the cost of inaction in product terms
  • I can identify which business-case components are strongest for a given situation
  • I can describe what a complete business case includes

Homework

Draft a short accessibility business case for one issue in your product area using at least two of these lenses: user reach, legal risk, brand value, efficiency gains, or cost of inaction.
