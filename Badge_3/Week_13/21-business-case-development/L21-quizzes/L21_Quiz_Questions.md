# L21 Quiz Questions: Business Case Development

**Lesson:** L21 - Business Case Development
**Badge Level:** Deliver
**Competency:** 3F
**Outcomes:** 3F.01, 3F.02, 3F.03

---

## Question 1
**Outcome:** 3F.01
**Type:** Recall
**Stem:**
> Which set of factors best forms the core of an accessibility business case?
- A. User reach, legal risk, brand value, and efficiency gains
- B. Typography, motion style, icon consistency, and layout density
- C. Engineering preference, visual polish, design trend, and novelty
- D. Personal opinion, team habit, release timing, and design taste
**Correct Answer:** A
**Rationale:** Badge 3 business cases should connect accessibility work to concrete organizational concerns, including user reach, legal risk, brand value, and efficiency.

## Question 2
**Outcome:** 3F.02
**Type:** Application
**Stem:**
> Which statement is the strongest example of communicating accessibility ROI to leadership?
- A. "Accessibility is important and we should care more about it."
- B. "Improving keyboard and screen reader support on this flow can reduce rework, lower support burden, and reduce compliance exposure in a core revenue path."
- C. "The design team prefers this change."
- D. "WCAG exists, so leadership should approve the work."
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** Strong ROI framing uses business-relevant language: risk, efficiency, support burden, and impact on important flows.

## Question 3
**Outcome:** 3F.03
**Type:** Recall
**Stem:**
> Which of the following is part of the cost of inaction on accessibility?
- A. Reduced need for maintenance
- B. Lower technical debt
- C. Legal exposure, customer churn, and accumulated rework
- D. Guaranteed product differentiation
**Correct Answer:** C
**Rationale:** The cost of inaction includes legal risk, user loss, support burden, and future remediation cost.

## Question 4
**Outcome:** 3F.03
**Type:** Application
**Stem:**
> A PM says accessibility work will slow roadmap progress. What is the strongest Badge 3 response?
- A. "Accessibility is morally important, so speed does not matter."
- B. "Deferring accessibility often increases rework and risk later, so early investment can reduce total delivery cost."
- C. "We should stop the roadmap entirely until everything is perfect."
- D. "Accessibility should be handled only after launch."
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** Badge 3 learners should be able to explain early accessibility investment as a cost-control and risk-reduction strategy.

## Question 5
**Outcome:** 3F.01 / 3F.03
**Type:** Scenario
**Stem:**
> A product team has low legal exposure but high user churn among users with disabilities. Which business-case approach is strongest?
- A. Lead with legal compliance risk and regulatory penalties
- B. Lead with user reach and product quality, supported by efficiency gains and rework reduction
- C. Present all four lenses equally without emphasizing any
- D. Focus only on brand reputation and skip metrics entirely
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** When legal exposure is low, user reach and product quality are more persuasive leads. A strong business case matches its emphasis to the situation.
