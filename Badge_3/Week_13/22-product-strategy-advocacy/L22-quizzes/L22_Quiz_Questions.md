# L22 Quiz Questions: Product Strategy Advocacy

**Lesson:** L22 - Product Strategy Advocacy
**Badge Level:** Deliver
**Competency:** 3G
**Outcomes:** 3G.01, 3G.02, 3G.03

---

## Question 1
**Outcome:** 3G.01
**Type:** Application
**Stem:**
> What does it mean to influence roadmap planning for accessibility?
- A. Wait until designs are final, then request fixes
- B. Make accessibility concerns visible during planning discussions about scope, timing, and priorities
- C. Focus only on design critique sessions
- D. Avoid planning conversations because accessibility is an implementation issue
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** Product strategy advocacy begins before detailed design. It involves surfacing accessibility risks and opportunities during planning.

## Question 2
**Outcome:** 3G.02
**Type:** Recall
**Stem:**
> Which statement best describes strong accessibility acceptance criteria?
- A. Broad, aspirational, and open to interpretation
- B. Specific, testable, and integrated into definition of done
- C. Informal notes that only the design team sees
- D. Optional suggestions for future releases
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** Acceptance criteria should be concrete enough to guide implementation and verification.

## Question 3
**Outcome:** 3G.03
**Type:** Scenario
**Stem:**
> A team is committing to a custom interaction pattern before evaluating whether a standard pattern could work. What is the strongest Badge 3 move?
- A. Wait until implementation starts before raising accessibility concerns
- B. Intervene early and raise the accessibility implications before the direction becomes expensive to reverse
- C. Ignore the issue because custom work is always more innovative
- D. Focus only on post-launch remediation
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** Badge 3 strategy work is about intervening before inaccessible direction solidifies into debt.

## Question 4
**Outcome:** 3G.01
**Type:** Application
**Stem:**
> Which situation best illustrates accessibility debt forming during planning?
- A. A team writes specific acceptance criteria before implementation
- B. A feature is scoped without any discussion of keyboard, AT, or reflow implications
- C. A PM asks for accessibility risks before locking scope
- D. A reviewer flags concerns during early concept work
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** Accessibility debt often starts when planning decisions are made without visibility into likely barriers or remediation cost.

## Question 5
**Outcome:** 3G.02
**Type:** Application
**Stem:**
> Which acceptance criterion is strongest?
- A. "The feature should be accessible."
- B. "Keyboard users can complete the primary task without a mouse, focus remains visible in all states, and error messages are programmatically associated with the relevant fields."
- C. "Follow WCAG where possible."
- D. "Design team to review accessibility later."
**Correct Answer:** B
**Rationale:** Strong acceptance criteria are explicit, testable, and connected to observable behavior.
