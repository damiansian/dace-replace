# L22: Product Strategy Advocacy - Learning Objectives

**Badge Level:** Deliver
**Delivery:** In-person (instructor-led)
**Duration:** ~30 minutes (part of Week 13 session: 2 lessons in 60 min)
**Outcomes Covered:** 3G.01, 3G.02, 3G.03
**Week:** 13 (04/07/26)

---

## Overview

This lesson teaches learners how to influence what gets built, not just how existing designs are reviewed. The focus is on roadmap influence, accessibility acceptance criteria, and early intervention before inaccessible design direction hardens into expensive implementation debt.

---

## Learning Outcomes

### 3G.01: Influence roadmap planning
**Outcome Statement:** Make accessibility concerns visible in product planning discussions.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand the strategic role of accessibility expertise in shaping what gets built
- Know how to influence roadmap planning by surfacing accessibility concerns in scope, budget, and timeline discussions
- Understand how your product fits into Adobe's broader accessibility strategy and compliance obligations

**Skills Component:**
- Influence roadmap planning by surfacing risks and opportunities early
- Identify accessibility risks early in product planning and roadmap discussions
- Connect product-level work to Adobe's organizational accessibility goals
- Adapt the accessibility message for different audiences: executives, PMs, engineers

**Application Component:**
- Participate in roadmap planning with accessibility considerations front and center
- Contribute to product strategy discussions with accessibility expertise

### 3G.02: Write accessibility acceptance criteria
**Outcome Statement:** Create specific and testable accessibility requirements for features.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Understand accessibility acceptance criteria and when to introduce them in the product development process

**Skills Component:**
- Write accessibility acceptance criteria that are specific, testable, and integrated into definition of done

**Application Component:**
- Write accessibility acceptance criteria for features in your product backlog

### 3G.03: Intervene early in design direction
**Outcome Statement:** Influence accessibility before significant design work is invested.

**Knowledge Component:**
- Know how to influence design direction before detailed design work begins
- Know when to push back on product decisions that create accessibility debt
- Know how to anticipate accessibility challenges in upcoming features before they are designed

**Skills Component:**
- Intervene early in design direction before significant work is invested in inaccessible approaches
- Anticipate accessibility challenges in upcoming features before they are designed
- Navigate pushback when accessibility requirements conflict with other product priorities

**Application Component:**
- Influence early design decisions before they become costly to change
- Identify upcoming features that will create accessibility challenges and intervene early
- Build relationships cross-functionally that enable you to influence product direction

---

## Lesson Checkpoints

### Checkpoint 1
- I can explain why roadmap influence matters more than late-stage correction
- I can adapt the accessibility message for different roles in a planning conversation

### Checkpoint 2
- I can write an accessibility acceptance criterion that is specific and testable

### Checkpoint 3
- I can identify one sign that a feature needs accessibility intervention before detailed design begins
