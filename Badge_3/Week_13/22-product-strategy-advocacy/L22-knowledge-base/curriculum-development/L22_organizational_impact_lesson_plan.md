# L22: Product Strategy Advocacy - Lesson Plan

**Course:** DACE
**Badge:** Deliver
**Delivery:** In-person (instructor-led)
**Duration:** ~30 minutes (Week 13 session: 30 min review + 60 min across L22, L23)
**Note:** Section 1 absorbs audience-adaptation content from L21 to keep framing skills where they are practiced.
**Outcomes:** 3G.01, 3G.02, 3G.03

---

## Lesson Overview

L22 teaches learners how to move accessibility earlier into the product lifecycle. The lesson focuses on roadmap influence, acceptance criteria, and intervention before inaccessible design patterns become expensive to reverse.

---

## Content Outline

### Opening: Why Early Influence Matters (3 min)
- Accessibility is cheapest and most effective when introduced before direction hardens

### Section 1: Roadmap Planning and Audience Adaptation (10 min)
**Outcomes:** 3G.01
- Surfacing accessibility risks in planning
- Connecting work to product and organizational goals
- Why planning visibility matters
- Adapting the message for different roles: executives, PMs, engineers

### Section 2: Acceptance Criteria (8 min)
**Outcomes:** 3G.02
- What makes criteria specific and testable
- Definition of done integration
- Feature-level examples

### Section 3: Early Intervention and Pushback (8 min)
**Outcomes:** 3G.03
- Early intervention vs late correction
- Recognizing accessibility debt in emerging concepts
- Handling tension with other priorities

### Close (2 min)
- Product strategy advocacy is about preventing debt, not just documenting it
