📊 Progress Check-In: What to Submit

Submit a brief status update showing where each component stands. This can be as simple as a table or a short paragraph per component.

For each of the six components, tell us:

  1. Status: Complete, in progress, or not started
  2. What exists: A one-sentence description of what you have so far
  3. What remains: What you still need to do before Week 15 submission

Example format:

Component Status What exists What remains
C1: Problem Definition Complete 12 findings classified by severity with summary statement Final review for clarity
C2: PEAS Reflection In progress Watched recorded session, notes taken Write the formal reflection using SBI framework

This is just an example — your status will reflect your actual progress.

📁 Portfolio Assembly: Start Now

If you have not already, start putting your components into your chosen portfolio format. The six components are the evidence — how you present them is your choice.

The portfolio should tell a story:

  1. Here is the problem my product faces (C1)
  2. Here is what I learned from observing expert practice (C2)
  3. Here is what I contributed to help my team (C3)
  4. Here is why the problem matters — legally and for the business (C4)
  5. Here is what I recommend we do about it (C5)
  6. Here are the relationships I built to make this happen (C6)

Acceptable formats: slide deck, written report, narrative website, video walkthrough, or a combined format. See the capstone page for full details on format options and the evaluation rubric.
