Course Syllabus

Design Accessibility Certified Expert (DACE) | Cohort 2 | April to August 2026

🎓 15-Week Program ⏱️ ~45 Total Hours 🏆 3 Progressive Badges

📋 Course Overview

The Design Accessibility Certified Expert (DACE) program is a comprehensive training initiative that builds accessibility expertise among Adobe Experience Designers. Through a progressive three-badge system, you will develop the knowledge and skills needed to create accessible digital experiences, test designs with assistive technology, conduct independent reviews, and mentor other designers.

📅 Weekly Schedule

Progress through three badges over 15 weeks to earn your DACE credential.

🔍

Discover Badge: Testing Foundation

Weeks 1 to 5

Build your foundation by learning to test designs against the Adobe Design Accessibility Checklist and create blueline annotations that communicate requirements to engineering.

Discover Badge weekly schedule: Weeks 1 to 5 covering accessibility testing fundamentals
Week Date Module Topic
Prep 4/1/26 to 4/27/26 Pre-read Async pre-read (~3 hrs)
1 4/28/26 L01, L02 Accessible Names; Headings

4 5/19/26 L07, L08 Landmarks & Navigation; Motion (reduced motion)
5 5/26/26 Badge 1 Discover Badge Assessment: Checklist-based testing practicum
🎧

Define Badge: Assistive Technology Expertise

Weeks 6 to 10

Understand why accessibility issues matter by learning how assistive technology actually works. By the end, you will be able to mentally model what a screen reader will announce just by examining a design.

Deliver Badge: Independent Review & Leadership

Weeks 11 to 15

📝 Assessment Structure

Competency-based assessments that reflect real-world practice.

During Each Badge (Open-Book)

  • Multiple choice questions in IAAP style
  • Hands-on practicums evaluating designs
  • Peer critique participation

Final DACE Credential (Closed-Book)

  • Domain 1: Design Accessibility Fundamentals (40%)
  • Domain 2: Assistive Technology & UX (35%)
  • Domain 3: Review Leadership & Compliance (25%)

🤝 Community Covenant: A Culture of Grace

Designing for accessibility requires us to confront our assumptions and learn new ways of thinking. To ensure a safe, protected, and productive learning environment, we agree to:

Grace for Mistakes: We acknowledge that we are all learning. We correct the work, not shame the designer.

Psychological Safety: You are safe to say "I don't know" or "I messed up." Questions are welcomed.

Respectful Critique: Feedback will be constructive, specific, and actionable. We challenge ideas, not people.

Person-First Respect: We respect diverse abilities and experiences, using inclusive language.
