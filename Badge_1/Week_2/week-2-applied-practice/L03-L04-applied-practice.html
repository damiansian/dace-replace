💡 Key Concepts to Apply

SC 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast: The "Required for Identification" Test

  • Critical question: "What visible information is required to identify this control?"
  • If text identifies the control's purpose, borders/fills may be incidental
  • If borders/fills are the only way to identify the control (icon-only buttons), they need 3:1
  • Disabled controls are exempt from contrast requirements

SC 3.3.2 Labels or Instructions: When Labels Are Sufficient

  • Critical question: "Can users understand what input is expected without losing context?"
  • Placeholder problem: Placeholder text disappears when typing, so it's not a persistent label
  • Contextual labels: Modal titles or adjacent content may provide sufficient labeling
  • Adjacent text or value displays can serve as labels when positioned correctly

Communicating with Stakeholders

  • Explain your reasoning, not just your conclusion
  • Reference WCAG criteria by name and number when relevant
  • Provide actionable recommendations when issues exist

📜 Related WCAG Success Criteria

Color Contrast

  • 1.4.3 Contrast (Minimum) (Level AA): Text has at least 4.5:1 contrast (3:1 for large text)
  • 1.4.11 Non-text Contrast (Level AA): UI components and graphics have at least 3:1 contrast
  • 1.4.1 Use of Color (Level A): Color is not the only means of conveying information

Forms and Labels

  • 3.3.2 Labels or Instructions (Level A): Labels or instructions are provided when content requires user input
  • 3.3.1 Error Identification (Level A): Input errors are automatically detected and described to the user
