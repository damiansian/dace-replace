### ✅ Grayscale Simulation Rationale

**User Requirement:** "Color simulation should only be achromatopsia/grayscale"

**L03 Implementation:**

- ✅ Primary method: Grayscale/achromatopsia simulation
- ✅ Rationale explained: Removes all hue, most stringent test
- ✅ Brief CVD context provided (protanopia, deuteranopia, tritanopia) for understanding
- ✅ Emphasis: Grayscale covers all CVD types, separate testing unnecessary
- ✅ Stark tool: Uses Achromatopsia simulation option

**Status:** ✅ Aligned with user requirements

---

### ✅ Disabled Button Treatment

**User Requirement:** "Need to cover disabled buttons"  
**User Clarification:** Focus on contrast requirements

**L03 Implementation:**

- ✅ Explicitly states disabled controls are exempt from contrast requirements (SC 1.4.3 and 1.4.11)
- ✅ Quotes WCAG Understanding document to reinforce exemption
- ✅ Emphasizes this point multiple times (common confusion point)
- ✅ Instructs students NOT to test disabled states
- ✅ Explains why: Different purpose (showing what exists but isn't available)

**Status:** ✅ Aligned with user requirements and WCAG guidance

---

### ✅ Button Boundary Measurement

**User Requirement:** "What to measure regarding boundaries on buttons"

**L03 Implementation:**

- ✅ Detailed explanation of measuring button boundaries (Section 2.4, 2.6 of Figma guide)
- ✅ Scenarios covered:
  - Button with visible border: Test border vs page background
  - Button with solid fill (no border): Test fill vs page background
- ✅ Diagram showing button anatomy
- ✅ Step-by-step measurement instructions with Stark
- ✅ Common mistake clarified: Testing text vs button (SC 1.4.3) vs testing button vs page (SC 1.4.11)

**Status:** ✅ Comprehensive coverage addressing user requirement
